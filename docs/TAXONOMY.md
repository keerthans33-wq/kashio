# Taxonomy

Deduction categories used in Kashio for the MVP.
These map to common ATO work-related expense categories for Australian employees.

> **Note on partial use:** If an item or subscription is used for both work and personal purposes, the user adds an estimated work-use percentage when claiming. Kashio records this percentage but does not calculate or validate it. The user is responsible for the accuracy of their claim.

---

## Categories

### 1. Work Software & Tools

**What it is:** Subscriptions or purchases of software and digital tools used for work.

**Examples:**
- Microsoft 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, Notion, Slack
- App purchases used for work tasks
- Domain names or hosting if required for work

**User must confirm:**
- The software is used for work, not personal use

**Evidence needed:**
- Receipt or invoice from the provider
- Subscription confirmation email

---

### 2. Office Supplies & Equipment

**What it is:** Items bought to do your job, used in a home office or at work.

**Examples:**
- Pens, notebooks, printer paper, folders
- A keyboard, mouse, monitor, or webcam
- A desk or chair (if used for work)

**User must confirm:**
- The item is used for work
- Items over $300 may need to be depreciated rather than claimed in full — user should check with their accountant

**Evidence needed:**
- Receipt or invoice showing item name, date, and amount
